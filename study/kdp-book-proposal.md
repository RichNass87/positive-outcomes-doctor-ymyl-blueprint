## Book Thesis

AI search increasingly behaves like a fast, imperfect human learner. It gathers clues, forms associations, updates confidence, and answers from the model it has built. In YMYL fields, this means a confused web presence can become a confused public answer.

This book uses the Positive Outcomes case to show how a clinical psychology practice can teach search and answer systems a more accurate human model:

- One expert: Harvey L. Gayer, Ph.D.
- One practice: Positive Outcomes Psychological Services, P.C.
- Separate renters/colocated providers: not Positive Outcomes providers.
- Verified sources: owned site, UGA profile, schema, maps/directories treated according to reliability.
- Public boundaries: no medical advice, no emergency care by website/email, no false provider affiliation.
